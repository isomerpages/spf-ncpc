<p>The National Crime Prevention Council (NCPC) held its annual Year-End
Crime Prevention Roadshow on 22 and 23 November 2025 at Our Tampines Hub.
The event was graced by Mr Goh Pei Ming, Minister of State,&nbsp; Ministry
of Home Affairs and Ministry of Social and Family Development.</p>
<p>This year’s roadshow focused on raising awareness about shop theft, outrage
of modesty, and voyeurism, while highlighting that crime prevention is
a shared responsibility.</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Launch of "We Jaga Together" Music Video</strong></h4>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 50%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Events/NCPC_Year_End_Roadshow_2025___2.png">
</div>
<p></p>
<p>A key highlight of the roadshow was the launch of the music video, <em>We Jaga Together</em>.</p>
<p></p>
<p>NCPC partnered with its Honorary Crime Prevention Ambassador, renowned
local director and actor Mr Jack Neo, to produce <em>We Jaga Together</em> to
deliver a clear message: crime prevention is a shared responsibility, and
low crime does not mean no crime. The video heavily incorporates AI-generated
visuals and musical elements to creatively communicate important public
messages.</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Delta Challenge: Art Against Scams – Prize Presentation</strong></h4>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Events/NCPC_Year_End_Roadshow_2025___3_min.png">
</div>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>The Delta Challenge: Art Against Scams, a joint
initiative by NCPC and the Singapore Police Force, engages youths through
creativity and community actions. Participants design murals that raise
awareness about scams and promote the 24/7 ScamShield Helpline (1799).</p>
<p></p>
<p>Winning artworks are being transformed into public murals across Singapore,
turning everyday spaces into powerful reminders to stay vigilant. The top
10 teams were also present at the roadshow to receive their prizes.</p>
